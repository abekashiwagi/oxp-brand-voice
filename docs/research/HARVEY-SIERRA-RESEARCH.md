# Research: Harvey AI & Sierra AI — Reference Models for a Multi-Family AI Platform

**Purpose:** Build a shared understanding of two AI-native platforms (Harvey, Sierra) as reference models for an AI-native multi-family platform that (1) improves operational efficiency by reducing human work, and (2) orchestrates humans and AI agents as a unified workforce.

## 1. Harvey AI

### 1.1 Overview

- **Domain:** Legal / professional services.
- **Positioning:** AI-powered platform for legal teams to research, draft, review, and collaborate—"engineered for every task" with domain-specific AI and workflows.
- **Scale (indicative):** 100K+ legal professionals; 200K+ queries/day; 1.3M+ files processed/day; 92% avg monthly usage (Harvey platform page, 2024–2025).

### 1.2 Core Capabilities (Product Pillars)

| Pillar | Role |
|--------|------|
| **Assistant** | Domain-specific AI: Q&A, document analysis, drafting. Every answer linked to source materials. |
| **Vault** | Secure storage, organization, bulk analysis of documents (workflows, review tables, Q&A, summarization). |
| **Knowledge** | Research across legal, regulatory, tax—multiple domains and sources. |
| **Workflows** | Pre-built and custom workflows (document analysis, translation, extraction). Workflow Builder lets firms design and deploy firm-specific workflows. |
| **Ecosystem** | Harvey inside existing tools: Word, Outlook, SharePoint, iManage, etc. Answers grounded in trusted sources (LexisNexis, NetDocuments, SharePoint, 400+ regional sources). |

### 1.3 Human–AI Model

- **Efficiency first:** "Streamline your entire process, from researching, drafting to reviewing" so teams "stay focused on delivering world-class work."
- **Human in the loop:** AI accelerates and augments; humans research, draft, and review with AI assistance. No replacement narrative—"scale expertise and impact."
- **Collaboration:** One platform for firms and corporations to collaborate securely across documents, workflows, and email—internally and externally.
- **Knowledge as moat:** Institutional knowledge (documents, data, third-party sources) unified in one environment "built for precision."
- **Control and trust:** Enterprise security (SOC2 II, ISO 27001, GDPR, etc.); answers grounded in designated sources.

### 1.4 Relevance to Multi-Family

- **Domain-specific assistant:** Like Harvey for legal, an assistant for multi-family (leases, policies, work orders, vendors, compliance) with answers tied to your documents and data.
- **Vault analogue:** Central, secure store for leases, addendums, work orders, vendor contracts, inspections—with bulk analysis and summarization.
- **Workflows:** Pre-built and custom workflows for leasing, maintenance, renewals, move-out, compliance, accounting—reducing manual steps.
- **Ecosystem:** Works inside tools teams already use (PMIS, email, calendar, accounting)—grounded in property/portfolio data.
- **Unified workforce:** Workflows and assistant reduce repetitive work; humans handle judgment, exceptions, and resident/owner relationships.

## 2. Sierra AI

### 2.1 Overview

- **Domain:** Customer experience / customer service (horizontal).
- **Positioning:** Conversational AI platform so businesses can deploy AI agents that interact with customers, solve problems, and take action—with seamless handoff to humans when needed.
- **Founders:** Bret Taylor (ex-Salesforce CEO), Clay Bavor (ex-Google). Launched 2024.
- **Notable metrics (examples):** WeightWatchers ~70% of sessions handled by agent, ~4.6/5 satisfaction; ADT 2M+ inquiries/month; outcome-based pricing.

### 2.2 Core Capabilities (Product Pillars)

| Pillar | Role |
|--------|------|
| **Agent (conversational)** | Autonomous agents that answer questions, recommend, process returns, manage subscriptions, etc., across chat, SMS, WhatsApp, email, voice, ChatGPT. |
| **Agent Studio** | No-code build and management of agents: journeys, knowledge, brand guidelines. |
| **Agent SDK** | Declarative goals and guardrails; composable skills, fine-tuning, developer tools. |
| **Voice** | AI phone support—inbound and outbound, natural speech, on-brand, with actions. |
| **Live Assist** | Real-time AI for human reps: next-step guidance, auto-drafted on-brand responses, one-click workflows, automatic capture/update of customer info. |
| **Insights** | Analytics, experimentation, observability for agent and process improvement. |

### 2.3 Human–AI Model

- **Agents first, humans when needed:** Agents handle a large share of sessions; escalation to humans with intent-based routing and conversation summaries so handoff is smooth and fast.
- **Unified workforce:** Same "agent" (goals, knowledge, brand) across channels; consistent experience whether the interaction is fully automated or human-assisted.
- **Augmenting humans (Live Assist):** When a human is in the loop, AI provides real-time guidance, drafts responses, and triggers workflows—improving consistency and reducing cognitive load.
- **Single agent build, many channels:** One agent definition across voice, chat, email, SMS, etc., with integration into existing contact center infrastructure.
- **Improvement loop:** Insights from every interaction (AI and human-assisted) used to improve agent behavior and outcomes.

### 2.4 Relevance to Multi-Family

- **Resident-facing agent:** One conversational agent for residents (maintenance, rent, lease questions, amenities, payments, renewals) across chat, SMS, voice, portal—reducing repetitive calls and emails.
- **Handoff and escalation:** Clear rules for when to escalate to leasing or maintenance; skills-based routing; full context and summary at handoff.
- **Live Assist for site staff:** Leasing and maintenance staff get real-time AI guidance, suggested replies, one-click actions (e.g., create work order, send notice), and auto-updated resident/property context.
- **Unified workforce:** Residents experience one “team” (AI + humans); operations see one queue and one set of insights for both agent-handled and human-handled interactions.
- **Efficiency + experience:** Fewer low-value repetitive tasks for humans; more time for high-touch and exception handling; better consistency and availability for residents.

## 3. Side-by-Side: Harvey vs Sierra

| Dimension | Harvey | Sierra |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| **Primary domain** | Legal / professional services | Customer service (horizontal) |
| **Primary “unit of work”** | Documents, matters, workflows | Conversations, sessions, actions |
| **Efficiency lever** | Research, draft, review, bulk doc work | Handle conversations and routine actions at scale |
| **Human–AI blend** | AI augments every step; humans approve and decide | Agents handle many sessions; humans take over when needed + Live Assist when they’re on the line |
| **Orchestration** | Workflows + Assistant + Vault + Knowledge | One agent across channels + handoff + Live Assist |
| **Trust / control** | Source-grounded answers, enterprise security | Escalation, guardrails, observability, compliance |

**For multi-family:** Harvey is the model for *internal* work (documents, workflows, knowledge); Sierra is the model for *resident-facing* conversations and *human–agent handoff*. A full platform would combine both: Harvey-like internal efficiency and Sierra-like resident-facing orchestration.

## 4. Multi-Family Context (Why This Fits)

- **Workload and staffing:** Many teams describe workload as "on the busy side"; staffing and workload management are top challenges; ~95 hours/month on manual processes (with ~61 saved by automation in one survey).
- **Resident expectations:** Maintenance and communication quality drive satisfaction and retention; repetitive resident inquiries and after-hours load are pain points.
- **Efficiency gains:** Manual maintenance requests, resident communication, and back-office tasks are prime candidates for automation and AI assistance.
- **Adoption:** Reluctance to change and adopt new software is real; solution must fit existing tools and workflows and demonstrate clear, low-friction value.

A Harvey+Sierra-style platform for multi-family would target: (1) **efficiency** via document/knowledge/workflow automation (Harvey-like), and (2) **unified workforce** via resident-facing agents and human augmentation/handoff (Sierra-like).

*Expand and prioritize as you refine the product.*
