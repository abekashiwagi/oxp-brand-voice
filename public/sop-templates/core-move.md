# **Move-In / Move-Out Procedures**

**\[Company Name\]**

## **Effective Date: \[Date\]**

## **1\. Purpose**

This SOP defines the procedures for resident move-in and move-out, including inspections, documentation, unit turnover, and coordination between leasing, maintenance, and accounting. Thorough documentation at both stages protects the company and the resident, prevents deposit disputes, and ensures units are consistently prepared.

## **2\. Scope & Applicability**

Applies to: Property managers, leasing staff, maintenance staff, AI agents, and any vendors involved in unit preparation or turnover.

Properties: All managed properties.

---

## **PART A: MOVE-IN**

### **3\. Pre-Move-In Preparation**

### **3.1 Unit Readiness (Make-Ready)**

Before a move-in date is confirmed, the unit must pass the make-ready checklist:

| Area | Standard |
| ----- | ----- |
| Cleaning | Professional deep clean completed; carpets shampooed or replaced; hard floors cleaned |
| Paint | Walls and trim painted or touched up; no marks, holes, or scuffs |
| Appliances | All appliances present, clean, and tested (stove, refrigerator, dishwasher, microwave, washer/dryer if provided) |
| Plumbing | All faucets, toilets, and drains functioning; no leaks |
| HVAC | Heating and cooling operational; filter replaced |
| Electrical | All outlets, switches, and fixtures working; bulbs replaced |
| Safety | Smoke detectors and CO detectors installed, tested, and documented; fire extinguisher (if provided) |
| Windows/doors | All windows and doors open, close, and lock properly; screens intact |
| Keys/access | All keys, fobs, and access credentials prepared |

### **3.2 Make-Ready Signoff**

1. Maintenance supervisor completes the make-ready checklist  
2. Property manager or leasing manager performs a quality inspection before the move-in date  
3. If the unit does not meet standards, create a work order for deficiencies and reschedule make-ready completion  
4. Unit is marked "ready" in Entrata only after signoff

### **4\. Move-In Day**

### **4.1 Move-In Inspection**

A detailed move-in inspection is completed on move-in day with the resident present:

1. Walk through the unit room by room with the resident  
2. Document the condition of every area using the Move-In Condition Report  
3. Photograph and/or video each room with timestamps — capture any existing imperfections (scuffs, stains, scratches, appliance condition)  
4. Test all appliances, faucets, lights, and fixtures during the walkthrough  
5. Both the resident and the property representative sign the report  
6. Provide the resident with a copy of the signed report and photos  
7. Upload the report and photos to Entrata attached to the resident/unit record

### **4.2 Key and Access Handoff**

1. Issue all keys, fobs, mailbox keys, and access credentials  
2. Document the number and type of each item issued  
3. Resident signs the key receipt  
4. Explain building access procedures, gate codes, amenity access, and parking

### **4.3 Move-In Orientation**

Provide the resident with:

* Welcome packet (community rules, emergency contacts, maintenance request instructions, amenity hours, parking rules, trash/recycling schedule, utility setup instructions)  
* Maintenance request process — how to submit requests (portal, phone, AI agent)  
* Emergency procedures — who to call for after-hours emergencies  
* Resident portal login — instructions for Entrata resident portal, online payments  
* Contact information — leasing office hours, management contact

### **4.4 Post-Move-In Follow-Up**

Within 7 days of move-in:

1. Contact the resident to confirm satisfaction and ask if any issues need attention  
2. Create work orders for any reported issues  
3. Document the follow-up in Entrata

## **PART B: MOVE-OUT**

### **5\. Move-Out Notice**

### **5.1 Receiving Notice**

1. Resident provides written notice to vacate per the lease and state law (typically \[30 days\] — verify per jurisdiction)  
2. Log the move-out date in Entrata  
3. Confirm receipt of notice in writing to the resident within 2 business days  
4. Begin marketing the unit with the projected availability date

### **5.2 Pre-Move-Out Communication**

Send the move-out instructions packet at least \[14 days\] before the move-out date:

* Cleaning requirements (specific and detailed — see checklist below)  
* Key and access credential return instructions  
* Forwarding address requirement (for deposit return and final correspondence)  
* Utility transfer/disconnection dates  
* Penalties for unreturned keys  
* Move-out inspection scheduling information  
* Reminder of lease obligations through the final day

### **5.3 Move-Out Cleaning Expectations**

Provide a clear checklist to the resident:

* Kitchen: appliances cleaned inside and out, countertops, cabinets, sink, floor  
* Bathrooms: toilet, tub/shower, sink, mirrors, floors, medicine cabinet  
* All rooms: vacuum or sweep all floors, clean windowsills, remove all personal property and trash  
* Walls: remove all nails, hooks, and adhesive; patch small holes if required by lease  
* Patio/balcony: cleared of personal items, swept  
* Garage/storage: emptied and swept

### **6\. Move-Out Inspection**

### **6.1 Conducting the Inspection**

1. Schedule the move-out inspection for the day of move-out or within 24 hours after the resident vacates  
2. Invite the resident to be present (required in some jurisdictions; best practice in all)  
3. Walk through the unit room by room using the Move-Out Condition Report  
4. Compare against the Move-In Condition Report and photos  
5. Photograph and/or video every area, especially any damage beyond normal wear and tear  
6. Document findings in detail — specific location, nature, and extent of each item  
7. Both parties sign the report if the resident is present  
8. Upload the report and photos to Entrata

### **6.2 Normal Wear and Tear vs. Damage**

Normal wear and tear (not chargeable to resident):

* Minor scuffs on walls, faded paint, small nail holes  
* Worn carpet in traffic areas (age-appropriate)  
* Minor scratches on hard floors  
* Faded or slightly stained countertops

Damage beyond normal wear (chargeable per lease and state law):

* Large holes in walls, unauthorized paint colors  
* Stained or burned carpet, pet damage  
* Broken fixtures, appliances, windows, or doors  
* Excessive filth requiring professional cleaning beyond standard turnover  
* Unauthorized alterations

Documentation is critical. Move-in photos establish baseline; move-out photos establish condition at vacate. Charges must be specific, itemized, and supported by evidence.

### **7\. Security Deposit Disposition**

Deposit handling follows SOP-008 (Security Deposit Handling). Summary:

1. Complete the damage assessment within \[3 business days\] of move-out  
2. Prepare the itemized deposit disposition statement per state law  
3. Return the deposit (or balance after deductions) within the state-mandated timeline: \[14–45 days depending on jurisdiction\]  
4. Send the statement and any refund to the forwarding address on file  
5. Store all documentation in Entrata

### **8\. Unit Turnover (Make-Ready for Next Resident)**

### **8.1 Turnover Timeline**

Target: Unit is rent-ready within 7 days of move-out.

| Day | Activity |
| ----- | ----- |
| 1 | Move-out inspection complete; damage assessment; turnover scope defined |
| 1–2 | Maintenance repairs and vendor coordination begins |
| 2–4 | Painting, carpet cleaning/replacement, appliance servicing |
| 4–6 | Deep cleaning, final repairs, punch list |
| 6–7 | Quality inspection and make-ready signoff |

### **8.2 Turnover Workflow**

1. Move-out inspection triggers the turnover work order in Entrata  
2. Maintenance supervisor reviews scope and assigns tasks  
3. Vendors scheduled as needed (paint, carpet, cleaning)  
4. Tasks completed and signed off individually  
5. Property manager or designee performs final quality inspection  
6. Unit marked "ready" in Entrata; available for leasing and move-in

## **9\. AI Agent Role**

The Leasing AI and Maintenance AI agents may:

* Answer resident questions about move-in procedures, documents needed, and orientation  
* Provide move-out instructions, deadlines, and cleaning expectations  
* Schedule move-out inspections  
* Create work orders for post-move-in issues reported by the resident  
* Provide turnover status updates to leasing staff

The agents must not:

* Make damage vs. normal wear determinations  
* Calculate or communicate security deposit deductions  
* Waive or modify move-out requirements

## **10\. Documentation and Recordkeeping**

* Move-in condition report and photos — retained for the duration of tenancy plus deposit disposition period  
* Move-out condition report and photos — retained for \[3–7 years\] per state law  
* Deposit disposition statement — retained per SOP-008  
* Key receipt — retained for duration of tenancy  
* All correspondence (notices, instructions, follow-ups) stored in Entrata

## **11\. KPIs**

| Metric | Target |
| ----- | ----- |
| Move-in inspection completed on move-in day | 100% |
| Move-out inspection within 24 hours of vacate | ≥ 95% |
| Post-move-in follow-up within 7 days | ≥ 90% |
| Unit turnover within 7 days | ≥ 85% |
| Deposit disputes (as % of move-outs) | ≤ 5% |
