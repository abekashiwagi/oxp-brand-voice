# **Lease Renewal Management**

**\[Company Name\]**

**Effective Date: \[Date\]**

## **1\. Purpose**

This SOP defines the procedures for managing the lease renewal process from initial outreach through execution of the renewed lease or notice to vacate. Renewals are a primary driver of occupancy, revenue stability, and resident retention. Consistent, timely, and well-documented renewal processes reduce vacancy, minimize turnover costs, and demonstrate the value of AI and human collaboration.

## **2\. Scope & Applicability**

Applies to: Property managers, leasing staff, Renewal AI agent, and any personnel involved in the renewal process.

Properties: All managed properties. Affordable/LIHTC properties have rent increase limitations per program rules.

Regulatory framework:

* State landlord-tenant law (notice period for rent increases and non-renewal; may require 30–90 days)  
* Local rent control / stabilization (where applicable — caps on increases, just-cause non-renewal)  
* Fair Housing Act (consistent renewal terms; no discriminatory non-renewal)  
* Lease terms (renewal options, automatic renewal clauses)

## **3\. Renewal Timeline**

### **3.1 Standard Timeline**

| Milestone | Timing | Owner |
| ----- | ----- | ----- |
| Renewal pricing set | 120 days before expiration | Property Manager / Revenue Manager |
| Renewal offer sent | 90 days before expiration | Renewal AI / Leasing team |
| Resident response deadline | 60 days before expiration (30 days to respond) | Resident |
| Follow-up if no response | 75 days before expiration | Renewal AI / Leasing team |
| Second follow-up | 65 days before expiration | Property Manager |
| Decision deadline | 60 days before expiration | Resident |
| Non-renewal notice (if needed) | Per state law (typically 30–60 days before expiration) | Property Manager |
| Renewal lease executed | 30 days before expiration | Leasing team |

### **3.2 Rent Control / Stabilized Properties**

For properties subject to rent control or stabilization:

* Verify the maximum allowable rent increase per the local ordinance  
* Provide required notice (often 30–90 days; may vary by increase amount)  
* Include any required disclosures about the tenant's rights  
* Document compliance with the local rent control board if required

## **4\. Renewal Pricing**

### **4.1 Pricing Strategy**

Renewal pricing is set by the Property Manager (or Revenue Manager / Regional Manager) and considers:

* Market rent for comparable units  
* Current resident's rent relative to market  
* Occupancy and budgeted rent growth targets  
* Turnover cost analysis (marketing, vacancy loss, make-ready, leasing commission vs. renewal in place)  
* Resident payment history and lease compliance  
* State and local rent increase restrictions

### **4.2 Pricing Approval**

* Standard renewal increases within the approved range require Property Manager approval  
* Increases above the approved range or significant concessions require Regional Manager approval  
* All pricing decisions documented in Entrata

### **4.3 Renewal Options**

Typically offer multiple lease term options:

| Term | Rent | Notes |
| ----- | ----- | ----- |
| 12-month renewal | $\[X,XXX\] | Standard |
| \[6-month\] renewal | $\[X,XXX\] | Short-term premium (typically 2–5% above 12-month) |
| Month-to-month | $\[X,XXX\] | Premium (typically 5–15% above 12-month) |

The number and terms of options are set per property policy.

## **5\. Renewal Outreach**

### **5.1 Initial Renewal Offer (90 Days)**

The renewal offer includes:

* Current lease expiration date  
* Renewal options (term, rent, effective date)  
* Any applicable concessions or incentives  
* Response deadline (30 days)  
* How to accept (portal, in person, or via the Renewal AI agent)  
* Contact information for questions

Delivery: Entrata portal notification, email, and/or mailed letter. AI agent may deliver via chat or SMS if the resident is engaged in a conversation.

### **5.2 Follow-Up (75 Days)**

If no response by the 75-day mark:

1. Renewal AI sends a follow-up message highlighting the offer and deadline  
2. Mention the benefits of renewing (e.g., no moving costs, community, locked-in rate)  
3. Ask if the resident has questions or concerns

### **5.3 Personal Follow-Up (65 Days)**

If still no response:

1. Property Manager or leasing agent makes a personal contact (phone call or in-person if on-site)  
2. Understand the resident's intent: renewing, considering, or planning to vacate  
3. Document the conversation in Entrata  
4. If the resident expresses concern about the rent increase, escalate to retention discussion (Section 7\)

## **6\. Renewal Acceptance and Execution**

### **6.1 Acceptance**

When the resident accepts a renewal option:

1. Generate the renewal lease in Entrata with the selected terms  
2. Send for electronic signature  
3. Resident and company representative sign  
4. Executed renewal stored in Entrata  
5. Confirm the new lease start date and any changes (rent, term)  
6. Update the resident portal with the new lease information

### **6.2 Execution Deadline**

The renewed lease should be fully executed at least 30 days before the current lease expiration. If signing is delayed, the leasing team follows up.

## **7\. Retention and Concessions**

### **7.1 When to Offer Concessions**

If a resident expresses intent to vacate due to the rent increase (or other concerns):

1. Escalate to the Property Manager  
2. Assess the resident's value: payment history, lease compliance, length of tenancy, market conditions  
3. Property Manager may offer a retention concession within the approved range (e.g., reduced increase, one-time credit, upgrade)  
4. Retention offers require Property Manager approval (above a threshold: Regional Manager)  
5. Document the concession and justification in Entrata

### **7.2 Concession Limits**

* Concessions must be offered based on documented business criteria (payment history, tenure, market conditions) — not on protected class  
* Maximum concession authority: Property Manager up to $\[XXX\]; Regional Manager above that  
* All concessions logged for audit

## **8\. Non-Renewal**

### **8.1 Resident-Initiated Non-Renewal (Notice to Vacate)**

1. Resident provides written notice per lease and state law  
2. Log the move-out date in Entrata  
3. Confirm receipt and provide move-out instructions per SOP-004  
4. Begin marketing the unit

### **8.2 Company-Initiated Non-Renewal**

If the company decides not to renew:

1. Ensure there is a lawful, non-discriminatory reason (and that the jurisdiction allows non-renewal without just cause, or that just cause exists)  
2. Property Manager approves the non-renewal decision  
3. Provide written notice per state and local law (\[30–90 days\] before expiration, depending on jurisdiction)  
4. Document the reason and all communications  
5. In jurisdictions with just-cause requirements, verify that the reason meets the statutory standard  
6. Store all documentation in Entrata

### **8.3 Fair Housing Compliance**

Non-renewals are subject to fair housing scrutiny. Ensure:

* The reason is documented and non-discriminatory  
* Non-renewal decisions are reviewed for patterns (e.g., disproportionate non-renewals of a protected group)  
* The Compliance Manager reviews a sample of non-renewals annually

## **9\. AI Agent Role**

The Renewal AI agent may:

* Deliver renewal offers and explain options (term, rent, concessions if pre-approved)  
* Answer questions about lease terms, renewal process, and deadlines  
* Send reminders and follow-ups  
* Collect the resident's intent (renew, undecided, or vacate)  
* Escalate concerns, objections, or retention requests to the Property Manager

The agent must not:

* Negotiate rent or offer concessions without human approval  
* Issue non-renewal notices  
* Make promises about pricing that are not in the approved offer  
* Communicate reasons for non-renewal

## **10\. Month-to-Month Transition**

If the resident neither renews nor provides notice to vacate, and the lease converts to month-to-month:

1. Notify the resident that the lease has converted to month-to-month at the applicable rate  
2. Continue to offer renewal options periodically  
3. Either party may terminate with the notice period specified in the lease or state law (typically 30 days)
