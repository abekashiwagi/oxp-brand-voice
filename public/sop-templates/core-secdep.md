# **Security Deposit Handling**

**\[Company Name\]**

## **Effective Date: \[Date\]**

## **1\. Purpose**

This SOP defines the procedures for collecting, holding, accounting for, deducting from, and returning security deposits in compliance with state and local law. Security deposits are one of the most litigated areas in landlord-tenant relationships. Strict adherence to this SOP protects the company from liability and ensures fair treatment of all residents.

## **2\. Scope & Applicability**

Applies to: Leasing staff (collection), property managers (deductions and disposition), accounting staff (processing), and AI agents (resident inquiries).

Properties: All managed properties. Every property must maintain a jurisdiction-specific addendum to this SOP reflecting the applicable state and local deposit rules.

Critical note: Security deposit laws are highly state-specific. The dollar amounts, timelines, and procedures in this SOP are templates — each property's addendum must specify the rules for its jurisdiction.

## **4\. Collection**

### **4.1 At Lease Signing**

1. Collect the security deposit per the lease agreement and state law  
2. Amount must not exceed the state maximum  
3. Provide the resident with a receipt showing:  
   * Amount collected  
   * Date  
   * Property and unit  
   * Where the deposit is held (if required by state law)  
4. Log the deposit in Entrata with the payment method, amount, and date

### **4.2 Pet Deposits and Additional Deposits**

* Pet deposit (where permitted): collected at lease signing or when a pet is added  
* Additional deposits (e.g., key deposit, last month's rent): only where permitted by state law  
* Service/assistance animals: No pet deposit may be charged for verified service or assistance animals (per FHA and SOP-009)  
* All deposits logged separately in Entrata

### **4.3 Deposit Holding**

* Deposits are held in a \[trust/escrow/designated\] account per state law  
* If state law requires interest, accrue and pay interest per the required schedule  
* Deposits are not commingled with operating funds (where required by law)

## **5\. During Tenancy**

### **5.1 Resident Inquiries**

Residents may inquire about their deposit at any time. The Payments AI agent may:

* Confirm the deposit amount on file  
* Explain the general deposit return process and timeline  
* Direct the resident to this SOP or the lease for specifics

### **5.2 Deposit Not Used During Tenancy**

The security deposit is not applied to rent during the tenancy unless:

* The resident and company agree in writing as part of the final month arrangement (and only where permitted by state law)  
* The resident has provided notice to vacate and both parties agree

## **6\. Move-Out and Disposition**

### **6.1 Damage Assessment**

Following the move-out inspection (per SOP-004):

1. Property Manager reviews the move-out condition report and photos  
2. Compares against the move-in condition report and photos  
3. Identifies items that are damage beyond normal wear and tear (see SOP-004 §6.2 for definitions)  
4. Obtains repair or cleaning estimates or actual invoices for each item  
5. Completes the assessment within \[3 business days\] of move-out

### **6.2 Allowable Deductions**

Deductions from the security deposit may include (per lease and state law):

* Unpaid rent (including prorated rent through the move-out date)  
* Late fees or other charges owed under the lease  
* Damage beyond normal wear and tear — specific, documented, and supported by photos and invoices  
* Cleaning (only if the unit was left in a condition beyond normal wear; must be documented)  
* Unreturned keys, fobs, or remotes (at replacement cost)

Not deductible:

* Normal wear and tear  
* Pre-existing conditions documented at move-in  
* Painting (unless damage beyond normal wear)  
* Carpet replacement due to age-appropriate wear

### **6.3 Itemized Statement**

Prepare an itemized security deposit disposition statement that includes:

* Resident name, unit, lease dates  
* Original deposit amount (including any additional deposits)  
* Each deduction listed separately with:  
  * Description of the item  
  * Location in the unit  
  * Amount charged  
  * Supporting documentation reference (invoice number, photo reference)  
* Total deductions  
* Balance due to resident (or balance owed by resident)  
* Interest owed (if required by state law)

### **6.4 Approval**

* Deductions under $\[XXX\]: Property Manager approves  
* Deductions over $\[XXX\] or full deposit retention: Regional Manager approves  
* All approvals documented in Entrata

### **6.5 Return Timeline**

1. Mail the itemized statement and any refund to the resident's forwarding address (or last known address if no forwarding address provided)  
2. Return within the state-mandated deadline (typically \[14–45 days\] after move-out — see the property's jurisdiction-specific addendum)  
3. Log the mailing date, method, and amount in Entrata  
4. If no forwarding address is on file, send to the unit address (it will be forwarded if the resident filed a forwarding order)

Failure to return within the deadline may result in penalties including forfeiture of the right to deduct, statutory damages (e.g., 2x–3x the deposit), and attorney fees.

## **7\. Disputes**

### **7.1 Resident Disputes a Deduction**

1. Resident submits a dispute in writing (letter, email, portal message)  
2. Property Manager acknowledges receipt within 48 hours  
3. Review the dispute against documentation:  
   * Move-in report and photos  
   * Move-out report and photos  
   * Invoices and estimates  
4. If the deduction is supported: respond in writing with the supporting evidence  
5. If the deduction is not adequately supported: adjust the deduction and issue the additional refund  
6. Document the dispute, review, and resolution in Entrata  
7. If unresolved: escalate to Regional Manager and/or legal counsel

### **7.2 Legal Claims**

If a resident files a legal claim (small claims court, attorney demand letter, or regulatory complaint):

1. Notify legal counsel immediately  
2. Preserve all records — do not alter or discard any documentation  
3. Provide legal counsel with the complete deposit file (move-in/out reports, photos, invoices, disposition statement, correspondence)  
4. Respond as directed by legal counsel within the required timeframe

## **8\. AI Agent Role**

The Payments AI and Leasing AI agents may:

* Confirm deposit amounts and explain the general return process  
* Explain allowable deductions at a high level (referencing this SOP)  
* Accept and log forwarding address updates  
* Answer questions about timeline and next steps

The agents must not:

* Calculate or communicate specific deduction amounts  
* Process refunds or credits  
* Make promises about deduction amounts or return timelines  
* Handle disputes (must escalate to Property Manager)

## **9\. Fair Housing Compliance**

* Deposit amounts, deduction criteria, and return processes must be applied consistently to all residents regardless of protected class  
* Do not apply different standards for damage assessment based on who the resident is  
* Document consistent application for audit

## **10\. Affordable / LIHTC Properties**

* Some affordable programs limit security deposit amounts (e.g., tied to program rent, not market rent)  
* Follow program-specific rules for deposit collection and return  
* Document compliance with program requirements
