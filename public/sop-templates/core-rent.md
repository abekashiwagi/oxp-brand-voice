# **Rent Collection & Delinquency Management**

## **1\. Purpose**

This SOP defines the procedures for collecting rent, managing late payments, enforcing delinquency policies, and offering payment plans. It ensures consistent treatment of all residents, compliance with lease terms and state law, and clear documentation of every step — from payment reminders through potential escalation to legal action.

## **2\. Scope & Applicability**

Applies to: Property managers, accounting staff, AI agents handling payment inquiries, and any personnel involved in rent collection and delinquency follow-up.

Properties: All managed properties. Affordable/LIHTC properties may have additional rent-related program requirements.

Regulatory framework:

* State landlord-tenant law (late fees, grace periods, notice requirements, accepted payment methods)  
* Fair Housing Act (consistent application of fees and enforcement)  
* Lease terms (due date, grace period, late fees, NSF fees, accepted payment methods)  
* State-specific rent payment protections (e.g., right to pay in certain forms, limits on late fees)

## **3\. Payment Terms and Methods**

### **3.1 Standard Terms**

| Term | Standard | Notes |
| ----- | ----- | ----- |
| Due date | 1st of each month | Per lease; may vary |
| Grace period | Through the \[3rd–5th\] of the month | Per lease and state law |
| Late fee | $\[XX\] or \[X%\] of monthly rent | Per lease; must not exceed state limit |
| NSF / returned payment fee | $\[XX\] | Per lease and state law |
| Accepted methods | Online (ACH, credit/debit via Entrata portal), check, money order, cashier's check | Per state law; some jurisdictions require acceptance of specific forms |

### **3.2 Online Payment Enrollment**

* Encourage all residents to enroll in autopay via the Entrata resident portal at move-in  
* Provide setup instructions in the welcome packet and via AI agent  
* Residents using autopay have significantly lower delinquency rates

## **4\. Payment Collection Timeline**

### **4.1 Pre-Due Date (Prevention)**

| Day | Action | Owner |
| ----- | ----- | ----- |
| 25th of prior month | Send payment reminder (portal notification, email, or AI message) | Automated / Payments AI |
| 28th–30th | Send second reminder to residents without autopay | Automated / Payments AI |

### **4.2 Due Date Through Grace Period**

| Day | Action | Owner |
| ----- | ----- | ----- |
| 1st | Rent due; online portal and autopay process | System |
| 2nd–\[5th\] | Grace period; no action unless resident reaches out | — |
| Grace period end | Late fee assessed automatically in Entrata | System |
| Grace period \+ 1 day | Late payment notice sent: amount due, late fee, payment options, deadline to avoid further action | Automated / Payments AI |

### **4.3 Early Delinquency (Days 6–14)**

| Day | Action | Owner |
| ----- | ----- | ----- |
| Day 6–7 | Personal outreach — phone call or direct message to resident | Property manager or AI agent |
|  | Offer to discuss payment options; document the conversation |  |
| Day 8–10 | If no response: second outreach attempt via different channel | Property manager |
| Day 10–14 | If no payment or arrangement: formal written demand (letter or Entrata notice) | Property manager |
|  | Include: total amount due, deadline, and consequences of non-payment |  |

### **4.4 Advanced Delinquency (Days 15–30)**

| Day | Action | Owner |
| ----- | ----- | ----- |
| Day 15 | If no payment or arrangement: escalate to Property Manager and/or Regional Manager | Accounting |
| Day 15–20 | Pay-or-quit notice prepared (per state law notice period and form) | Property manager with legal review |
|  | Notice served per state requirements (personal delivery, posting, certified mail) |  |
| Day 20–30 | If notice period expires and no payment: refer to SOP-010 (Eviction & Legal Notice Procedures) | Property manager / legal |

### **4.5 Ongoing Delinquency (30+ Days)**

* If a payment plan is in place, follow the plan schedule (Section 6\)  
* If no plan and no payment: proceed with legal/eviction process per SOP-010  
* Report to collections agency if the resident has vacated with a balance (per company policy and state law)

## **5\. Late Fee Policy**

### **5.1 Assessment**

* Late fees are assessed automatically in Entrata after the grace period ends  
* Late fee amount per the lease agreement, not exceeding the state maximum  
* Late fees apply to all delinquent residents consistently — no selective enforcement

### **5.2 Waiver Requests**

* Late fee waivers must be approved by the Property Manager (or designated authority)  
* Waivers may be granted for documented extenuating circumstances (e.g., verified payroll error, documented emergency)  
* Maximum: \[1\] waiver per resident per \[12-month\] period (unless approved by Regional Manager)  
* All waivers are documented in Entrata with the reason and approver  
* AI agents must not waive fees; they escalate waiver requests to the Property Manager

### **5.3 NSF / Returned Payment**

* NSF fee assessed per lease and state law  
* Resident notified immediately  
* If \[2+\] returned payments in \[12 months\]: require certified funds for remaining lease term (where permitted by state law)

## **6\. Payment Plans**

### **6.1 Eligibility**

Payment plans may be offered when:

* The resident has a balance due and demonstrates willingness to pay  
* The resident provides a documented reason for the hardship (e.g., job loss, medical)  
* The plan can bring the account current within \[30–60 days\]  
* The resident is not already on an active payment plan

### **6.2 Payment Plan Terms**

1. Property Manager (or designee) negotiates terms with the resident  
2. Plan is documented in writing and signed by both parties  
3. Terms include: total balance, installment amounts, due dates, and consequences of default  
4. Plan is logged in Entrata and monitored  
5. If the resident defaults on the plan, the standard delinquency process resumes from the current balance

### **6.3 AI Agent Role**

The Payments AI agent may:

* Inform residents of their balance, due dates, and payment methods  
* Explain late fees and grace periods per the lease  
* Escalate payment plan and waiver requests to the Property Manager  
* Send payment reminders and late notices

The agent must not:

* Negotiate payment plans or waive fees  
* Threaten legal action or eviction  
* Share another resident's payment information  
* Make promises about fee waivers or exceptions

## **7\. Partial Payments**

* Acceptance of partial payments may waive the right to pursue eviction for that period in some jurisdictions  
* Property Manager must consult with legal counsel before accepting partial payment during an active pay-or-quit notice period  
* If partial payments are accepted: document in writing that acceptance does not waive the right to pursue the remaining balance (where legally permissible)  
* Log all partial payments in Entrata with notes

## **8\. Documentation and Recordkeeping**

* All payment records are maintained in Entrata (payments, credits, fees, adjustments, payment plans)  
* All notices (reminders, late notices, demands, pay-or-quit) are stored in Entrata with delivery confirmation  
* All conversations regarding delinquency (AI agent logs, phone notes, email) are documented  
* Retain for the duration of tenancy plus \[3–7 years\] per state law and company policy  
* Records must be available for audit and any legal proceedings

## **9\. Fair Housing Compliance**

* Late fees, notices, and enforcement actions must be applied consistently to all residents  
* No preferential treatment or harsher enforcement based on protected class  
* If a resident requests a reasonable accommodation related to payment (e.g., alternative payment date due to disability-related income timing), process per SOP-009  
* Document consistent application for audit
