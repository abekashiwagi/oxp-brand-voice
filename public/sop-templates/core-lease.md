# **Lease Execution & Administration**

**\[Company Name\]**

**Effective Date: \[Date\]**

## **1\. Purpose**

This SOP defines the procedures for preparing, executing, managing, and amending residential lease agreements. It ensures all leases are consistent, compliant with fair housing and state landlord-tenant law, properly documented, and stored in the system of record (Entrata).

## **2\. Scope & Applicability**

Applies to: All leasing staff, property managers, AI agents involved in the leasing process, and any personnel authorized to execute or modify lease agreements.

Properties: All managed properties. Affordable/LIHTC properties follow additional program-specific lease requirements.

Regulatory framework:

* Fair Housing Act — nondiscriminatory terms and conditions  
* State landlord-tenant law — required disclosures, prohibited clauses, notice requirements  
* Local ordinances — rent control, just-cause provisions, required addenda  
* Program-specific rules (LIHTC, HUD-assisted) where applicable

## **3\. Lease Preparation**

### **3.1 Standard Lease Templates**

* All properties use company-approved lease templates stored in the document vault  
* Templates are reviewed annually by legal counsel and the Compliance Manager  
* Templates include all state-required disclosures and addenda for the property's jurisdiction  
* No modifications to standard lease language without approval from the Property Manager or above

### **3.2 Lease Assembly**

After application approval (per SOP-002), prepare the lease within 24 hours:

1. Generate the lease in Entrata using the approved template for the property and unit type  
2. Populate fields: resident name(s), unit number, lease start/end dates, monthly rent, security deposit, concessions (if any), parking/storage, pet addendum (if applicable)  
3. Attach required addenda:  
   * Lead-based paint disclosure (pre-1978 buildings)  
   * Mold disclosure (where required by state)  
   * Bed bug addendum (where required)  
   * Crime-free / nuisance addendum (where applicable and lawful)  
   * Utility addendum (RUBS or sub-metering disclosure)  
   * State-specific disclosures (vary by jurisdiction)  
   * Reasonable accommodation notice  
4. Review: Leasing manager or authorized personnel reviews the assembled lease for accuracy and completeness before sending to the applicant

### **3.3 Move-In Cost Summary**

Provide a clear, itemized move-in cost summary with the lease, including:

* First month's rent (or prorated amount)  
* Security deposit  
* Pet deposit or pet rent (if applicable)  
* Administrative fees (if applicable and lawful in the jurisdiction)  
* Any other charges

## **4\. Lease Execution**

### **4.1 Delivery**

1. Send the lease package to the applicant via Entrata's electronic signature platform (or approved alternative)  
2. Provide a clear deadline for signing: 72 hours from delivery (or per company policy)  
3. Include instructions for reviewing, signing, and returning the lease  
4. Notify the applicant that they may request clarification on any terms

### **4.2 Signing**

1. All adult occupants listed on the lease must sign  
2. Authorized property representative signs on behalf of the company  
3. Executed lease is stored in Entrata and a copy is provided to the resident  
4. Lease execution triggers the move-in workflow (SOP-004)

### **4.3 Lease Signing Deadline**

If the applicant does not sign within the deadline:

1. Send a reminder at the 48-hour mark  
2. If unsigned at 72 hours, contact the applicant to confirm intent  
3. If no response within \[24 additional hours\], the unit is released back to availability and the applicant is notified in writing  
4. Application may be reactivated if the applicant re-engages and the unit is still available

## **5\. Lease Terms — Consistency Requirements**

### **5.1 Standard Terms**

All residents in comparable units receive the same standard lease terms. Non-standard terms (e.g., shorter lease length, special concessions, early termination clauses) require documented business justification and manager approval.

### **5.2 Prohibited Variations**

Lease terms must never vary based on:

* Protected class (per SOP-001)  
* Personal relationship with staff  
* Verbal agreements not documented in the lease

### **5.3 Concessions**

If concessions are offered (e.g., free month, reduced deposit):

* The concession must be available to all applicants meeting the same criteria during the promotional period  
* Document the concession in the lease addendum  
* Log in Entrata for reporting and audit

## **6\. Lease Amendments and Modifications**

### **6.1 When Amendments Are Needed**

* Occupant changes (adding or removing a leaseholder)  
* Transfer to a different unit  
* Lease term extension or modification  
* Rent adjustment outside of standard renewal (rare; requires approval)  
* Reasonable accommodation requiring a lease change (per SOP-009)

### **6.2 Amendment Process**

1. Resident or staff requests the amendment in writing  
2. Leasing manager reviews and approves or escalates for further approval  
3. Amendment document is prepared using the approved template  
4. All parties sign the amendment  
5. Amendment is attached to the lease in Entrata  
6. Processing target: 48 hours from approval to execution

### **6.3 Unauthorized Changes**

No verbal agreements or side letters. All lease modifications must be in writing, signed by all parties, and recorded in Entrata. Staff who make unauthorized changes are subject to corrective action.

## **7\. Early Termination**

### **7.1 Resident-Initiated**

1. Resident submits a written request for early termination  
2. Leasing manager reviews lease terms (early termination clause, if any)  
3. If the lease allows early termination with a fee, provide the resident with the fee amount and required notice period  
4. If the lease does not include an early termination clause, discuss options (e.g., subletting where permitted, lease buyout) and escalate to the Property Manager  
5. Document the agreement in an amendment or termination agreement  
6. Process security deposit per SOP-008

### **7.2 Company-Initiated (Non-Renewal)**

1. Provide written notice per state and local law (notice period varies: 30–90 days depending on jurisdiction)  
2. Document the reason for non-renewal (must be non-discriminatory and lawful)  
3. In jurisdictions with just-cause requirements, ensure the reason meets the statutory standard  
4. Store the notice and documentation in Entrata

## **8\. Lease Renewal**

Lease renewals are governed by SOP-007 (Lease Renewal Management). At a high level:

1. Renewal outreach begins \[60–90 days\] before lease expiration  
2. Renewal terms (rent, lease length) are set by the Property Manager per the approved rent schedule  
3. Residents receive a written renewal offer with a response deadline  
4. Executed renewals are stored in Entrata as a new lease term

## **9\. AI Agent Role in Lease Administration**

The Leasing AI and Renewal AI agents may:

* Answer resident questions about lease terms, move-in costs, and lease dates  
* Provide status updates on lease preparation and signing  
* Send reminders about signing deadlines  
* Explain standard lease provisions using approved language from this SOP

The agents must not:

* Modify lease terms or make concession offers without human approval  
* Execute lease amendments  
* Provide legal advice or interpretation beyond explaining standard lease provisions  
* Approve early termination requests

## **10\. Documentation and Recordkeeping**

* All executed leases and amendments stored in Entrata and retained for the duration of the tenancy plus \[3–7 years\] per state law  
* All correspondence related to lease preparation, signing, amendments, and termination retained in Entrata  
* AI interaction logs for lease-related conversations retained per platform policy
